How can I get involved?

Find some friends and form your game making team. There are so many roles available, anyone can contribute!

Game design

Design the rules of your game. How do you win? How do you lose? Are we playing for points or for playing for time? The choice is yours!

Artwork

What will your game look like? Pixel art? Geometric shapes? Like a post-impressionism painting? You'd be in charge of the visual direction of your game.

Music and sound

Sound makes your game world come to life. Provide the soundtrack and ambience for your game, what do the characters sound like? What does the world sound like?

Coding

Write the code that will drive the logic of your game. How do we control the game?

Marketing

Why should people play your game? Can you entice them with an amazing trailer for your game?

Story

Engage your players with an exciting storyline. Who are the characters in your game? What motivates them?

Paying the rent as an indie developer

Dr Sean Walton

Making a profit from an indie game is challenging and requires hard work and a bit of luck. So, is it possible to pay the rent (and eat) while making indie games which have not blown up on Steam? In this talk Sean will share his experience making indie games for the past 5 years alongside a full-time job. What are the pros and cons of running an indie studio on the side, and is it a good idea?
