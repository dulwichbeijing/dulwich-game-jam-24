Online game making workshop

Participate in a 1 hour game making workshop led by experts from Ubisoft's China Studios.

  • Benjamin Gallier - Game Design, Ubisoft Shanghai
  • Francois Remacle - Senior Lead Programmer, Ubisoft Shanghai
  • Gabriel Tan - Art Director, Ubisoft Chengdu

Q+A sessions

Session 1 - Game Design

30 minute session to discuss all things game design with Benjamin Gallier.

Session 2 - Programming

30 minute session to have your coding questions answered by Francois Remacle.

Session 3 - Art For Games

30 minute session to speak with Gabriel Tan to learn about his processes for creating game artwork.

Competition rules

You and your teammates will need to create a game relating to a top secret theme.

  • Teams can consist of 1 to 8 students.
  • All members in a team must be part of the same age group. For the 2024 Dulwich Game Jam there are 3 different age groups:
    • Junior school (Y5-Y6)
    • KS3 (Y7-Y9)
    • KS4/5 (Y10-Y13)
  • All team members must be enrolled in an EiM family school for the 2023/2024 academic year.
  • Students can only be a member of 1 team.
  • Students need to include a credits (either in game or as a separate text file) explaining who was responsible for each part of the project.
  • Students are encouraged to create the majority of art and music for their game. If students use any assets found online, full credit must be given to the original author(s) as part of the gameâ€™s credits.
  • When you submit your work you need to upload copies of all code, artwork, music, sound effects, etc. you used to create your game.
  • Students need to upload their work by the Sunday lunchtime deadline to be eligible for prizes and swag.
